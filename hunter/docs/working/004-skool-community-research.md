# Skool Community Research: Tech Education
**Date**: 2026-02-08
**Domain**: DevOps / Programming / Cybersecurity Education

## Key Finding: DevOps on Skool Is Severely Underserved

Only ONE DevOps-specific community exists (CoderCo at $199-799/mo). Compare to hundreds of business/marketing communities. Massive gap.

## Platform Economics

- Creator cost: $99/month (Pro plan, 2.9% transaction fee)
- Median paid community price across Skool: $47/month
- Top communities: $3,400-$335,000/month revenue
- Many creators earn $5K-$20K/month with just 100-200 members
- Average churn: ~18% monthly → avg member tenure 5.5 months
- Good churn: <10% → avg member tenure 10+ months

| Tier | Price/mo | Value Proposition |

## Community Impact on Course Completion

- Self-paced course alone: 5-15% completion
- Course + community: 30-60% completion
- Course + community + live cohort: 70-85% completion
- Students in communities are 5x more engaged, 16x more likely to complete

## Skool Limitations for Tech

- No code formatting / syntax highlighting
- Single-threaded feed (no channels/rooms)
- No GitHub integration
- "Skool bro" reputation may turn off technical audience

## Recommended Model

1. Free Skool/Discord as top-of-funnel
2. Paid tier at $49-99/month (courses, weekly calls, curated paths)
3. Discord supplement for real-time technical Q&A (code blocks, channels)
4. Challenge/cohort launches ($97-197) for 20-40% conversion to ongoing
5. Premium tier ($199+/month) for 1:1 mentoring

## Math

- 100 members × $49/mo = $4,900/mo
- 200 members × $49/mo = $9,800/mo
- Break-even on Skool Pro: 2-3 members
